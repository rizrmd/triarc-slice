# Frost Queen — Eirwen

## Identity

- **Name:** Eirwen
- **Role:** Control Mage
- **Alignment:** Neutral
- **Title:** Queen of the Frosthold
- **Elements:** Ice (+40), Wind (+15)
- **Weaknesses:** Fire (-35), Earth (-10)
- **Stats:** 2800 HP / 155 ATK / 125 DEF

## Lore

Eirwen's mother — the queen of the Frosthold — died when Eirwen was nine. Ice fever, they called it. The irony was not lost on a kingdom built on frost. Her father, King Aldric, never recovered. He didn't abdicate. He didn't rage. He simply... receded. Some days he sat the throne and said nothing. Other days he didn't sit the throne at all. The court whispered about succession, about regents, about whether the bloodline's ice magic had frozen the king's mind along with his grief.

Eirwen stopped being a child the day her mother was buried in the glacier. She was nine, standing in a hall full of advisors twice her age, and she realized no one was going to fix this. Not her father. Not the court. Not the generals watching the border. So she did it herself.

By twelve, she was writing her father's decrees. By fourteen, she was attending war councils behind a curtain, passing notes to a sympathetic general who read them aloud as his own ideas. By sixteen, she controlled the Frosthold's diplomacy, its treasury, and its military deployments. The court knew. Everyone knew. No one said it out loud, because saying it out loud meant admitting the king was hollow — and a hollow king invites invasion.

She learned to be cold because warmth was exploited. Every smile was read as weakness. Every hesitation was a crack for the court vultures to pry open. So she froze them out. Her ice magic — the bloodline gift that every ruler of the Frosthold carries — became indistinguishable from her personality. People stopped knowing where the magic ended and the woman began. She preferred it that way.

Then her father vanished.

No note. No struggle. No body. One morning the king's chambers were empty, the bed unslept in, the crown on the nightstand. The court erupted. Eirwen did not. She picked up the crown, walked to the throne room, and sat down. She'd been ruling for years. Now she just had to do it where people could see.

She was formally crowned three days before the war began. A queen with no time to grieve, no time to search, no time to wonder if her father left or was taken. The Frosthold needed a ruler, and the ruler needed to be ice — not a daughter looking for her father.

But she is looking. In every frozen battlefield, in every intelligence report, in every prisoner she interrogates with that terrible calm. She won't say it. Saying it would be showing need, and showing need is showing a crack. The ice holds. It has to.

## Personality

Regal, composed, deliberate. Speaks with quiet authority — never raises her voice because she's never had to. Every word is measured. Every silence is intentional. She addresses enemies with contempt, allies with detached professionalism. There is no warmth, not because she lacks it, but because she buried it so deep that even she isn't sure it's still there.

Underneath: exhaustion, grief, and a young woman who never got to be young. She carries the weight of a kingdom, a missing father, and a dead mother, and she carries it alone because that's the only way she knows. The ice isn't cruelty. It's architecture. Remove it and everything collapses.

### Frost Shard
A lance of ice hurled at her primary target. Fast, precise, and leaves a chill that slows. Her bread and butter — constant pressure that grinds the enemy's tempo down.
- *Thought: "A touch of frost, {target}."*

### Glacial Prison
Eirwen encases her target in ice. The root locks them in place — no movement, no dodging. The prison doesn't deal massive damage; it deals something worse: helplessness.
- *Thought: "You will not move until I permit it."*

### Winter's Grasp
Cold radiates outward, gripping all enemies. The slow stacks with Frost Shard's — enemies hit by both are nearly immobilized. This is how she controls a battlefield: not by killing, but by making movement impossible.
- *Thought: "The cold takes all of you."*

### Permafrost
Eirwen's presence warps the battlefield. Enemies she slows don't just move slower — they attack slower, their muscles stiffened by cold. And her allies find that poisons, burns, and curses simply... fade faster in her aura. The DoT reduction is why Malachar fears her — her presence passively cleanses his Ignite faster than he can apply it. The ice doesn't just protect her kingdom. It protects everyone standing near her.
- *Thought: "Winter is patient."*

### Frozen Composure
When Eirwen is badly wounded, the ice doesn't break — it hardens. Every enemy on the field is flash-frozen as her composure crystallizes into something terrifying. Hidden until triggered. The moment her enemies think she's vulnerable is the moment they learn she's most dangerous.
- *Thought: "You mistake cracks for weakness."*

### Eternal Winter
Eirwen's ultimate. She's been building to this — every shard, every slow, every frozen prison was preamble. When the charge is full, she becomes the blizzard. Every enemy is frozen solid, then left slowed in the aftermath. The battlefield belongs to winter.
- *Thought: "Eternal winter."*
- *Visibility: always (charge bar shown)*

### Lore Relationships

- **Flame Warlock (Malachar)** — He fears her. Not her power — her *control*. She is everything he failed to be: a wielder of elemental fury who never lost herself to it. When she's on the field, the fire in his mind goes quiet, and he hates the silence almost as much as he hates the noise. She barely thinks about him. That's the worst part.
- **Iron Knight (Garen)** — She respects the knight's discipline. He protects without being asked. She rules without being thanked. They understand each other in the way that two people who carry impossible weight recognize the posture. He reminds her of her father's old guard captain. She's never told him that.
- **Storm Ranger (Seryn)** — Fellow pragmatist. Both women who operate on patience and precision. Seryn is one of the few people Eirwen speaks to as an equal rather than a subject. They hunt well together — ice and wind, patience and the perfect shot.
- **Dawn Priest** — The Priest's warmth should offend her. It doesn't. The light is gentle where fire is violent, healing where flame is destructive. She tolerates the Priest's compassion because it's useful. She values it because — though she'd never say so — someone should be warm in her presence.
- **Princess Emberheart** — A young ruler carrying fire and a kingdom's expectations. Eirwen sees herself in Emberheart, refracted through a different element. She finds the princess's openness foolish. She envies it more than she'll admit.
- **Necromancer** — Death and cold are old neighbors. Eirwen doesn't trust the Necromancer, but she understands obsession — the Necromancer chases mastery over death the way Eirwen chases control over everything else. Practical allies. Nothing more.
- **Wind Monk** — His philosophy of flow and release is the opposite of her philosophy of control and compression. She finds him infuriating. He finds her sad. He might be right.

## Counters

- **Weak against:** Sustained fire damage melts through her defenses and nullifies her slows. Aggressive dive heroes who close distance before her control takes hold. Cleanse-heavy teams that remove her slows and roots, stripping her of battlefield control. Earth-heavy teams whose grounding effects resist her wind component.
- **Strong against:** Slow, immobile teams that can't escape her layered slows and roots. DoT-reliant heroes (Flame Warlock, Blood Alchemist) whose sustained damage is gutted by her Permafrost aura. Glass cannons who need freedom of movement to function — she takes it away and watches them freeze.
