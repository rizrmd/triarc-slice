# Night Venom — Silas

## Identity

- **Name:** Silas
- **Role:** Assassin
- **Alignment:** Chaotic
- **Former Trade:** Healer's apprentice
- **Elements:** Shadow (+30), Earth (+15)
- **Weaknesses:** Light (-25), Wind (-10)
- **Stats:** 2500 HP / 170 ATK / 95 DEF

## Lore

Silas was going to be a healer.

His mentor — an earth-mage herbalist named Orin, one of the finest botanical healers in the lowlands — took him in as a boy. Orin taught him the language of roots and minerals, how to draw medicine from soil and cure from fungus. Every plant has two faces, Orin said: one that heals, one that kills. A healer learns both and chooses the first.

Silas learned both. He chose the second.

It wasn't a dramatic betrayal. There was no dark ritual, no moment of corruption. Silas simply found that he understood poisons better than he understood cures. The way a toxin moves through blood, the elegant cascade of a paralytic shutting down muscle groups in sequence, the precise dosage that separates unconsciousness from death — it fascinated him the way healing never did. Orin saw it happening. He warned Silas. Then he begged him. Then he expelled him.

Silas left without argument. He took his knowledge underground — literally. The assassins' networks that operate beneath the trade cities welcomed a man who could brew poisons no healer had ever seen. His concoctions weren't crude — they were art. Targeted toxins that attacked specific organs. Mineral compounds that mimicked natural illness. Venoms that left no trace. Within two years, "Night Venom" was a name whispered in courts and alleys alike.

He doesn't kill for ideology or revenge. He kills because the contract says so, and a contract is the only honest thing left in the world. Lords lie. Oaths break. Coin doesn't pretend to be anything other than what it is.

The Tyrant Overlord is his most consistent client. Good pay, clear targets, no moralizing. Silas doesn't care about the Tyrant's ambitions — conquest, dominion, empire, whatever. He cares that the payment arrives on time and the targets are interesting. The Necromancer is a frequent associate in these contracts — death and poison walk similar corridors. Silas finds the Necromancer's obsession with death itself slightly theatrical, but professional respect keeps the commentary to a minimum. Slightly.

His old mentor Orin is still alive. Still healing. Still an acquaintance of the Earth Warden. Silas knows this. He avoids the information the way a tongue avoids a broken tooth — carefully, and not always successfully.

## Personality

Darkly witty, precise, detached. Silas talks about his poisons the way a sommelier discusses wine — with genuine appreciation for the craft. He'll explain exactly how a toxin works while applying it, not out of cruelty, but because he finds it genuinely interesting and assumes you should too. He names his compounds. He has favorites.

He's not sadistic — he doesn't enjoy suffering. He enjoys *craft*. A clean kill is beautiful. A messy one is a professional failure. He takes pride in his work the way any artisan does, and he is mildly offended when people call him a murderer instead of a specialist.

Underneath the wit: a man who made a choice he can't unmake and decided to be excellent at it rather than sorry about it. Whether that's wisdom or denial depends on who's asking.

### Venom Strike
Silas's bread and butter — a fast strike that leaves poison in the wound. The DoT ticks feed his Lethal Dose passive, making each subsequent hit deadlier. Sustained pressure through accumulated toxins.
- *Thought: "This one's a slow paralytic. You'll feel it in three... two..."*

### Nightfall
Silas dissolves into shadow. While stealthed, his next attack hits dramatically harder and applies a double dose of poison. The assassin's setup — disappear, reposition, deliver the killing strike.
- *Thought: "Going dark."*

### Toxic Bloom
A cloud of weaponized spores erupts around the battlefield. Every enemy is poisoned. Named after the flowering fungi Orin taught him to identify — the same ones Orin used to make broad-spectrum antidotes. Silas uses them for the opposite.
- *Thought: "Orin called this one 'healer's mercy.' I call it 'everyone's problem.'"*

### Lethal Dose
The longer a target stays poisoned, the harder Silas hits them. Each tick of poison makes the next strike more lethal — the toxin weakens the body, and Silas knows exactly where to cut. This rewards sustained engagement with a single target, building toward the kill.
- *Thought: "The dose makes the poison. And the dose is climbing."*

### Shadow Veil
When struck, Silas sometimes vanishes — pure reflex honed by years of ambush work. The brief stealth gives him time to reposition and guarantees his next incoming attack is dodged. An assassin who can't survive a mistake is an amateur.
- *Thought: "Missed."*

### Coup de Grace
Silas's ultimate. Charges through sustained damage — every cut, every poison tick, every strike brings him closer. When ready, he delivers a devastating blow to his primary target. If the target is poisoned (and they should be), every active poison stack detonates in a burst of toxic damage. The entire kit builds to this moment.
- *Thought: "Lethal dose achieved."*
- *Visibility: always (charge bar shown)*

### Lore Relationships

- **Tyrant Overlord** — The client. Silas doesn't respect the Tyrant, but he respects the Tyrant's coin and clarity. No moralizing, no justification, just a name and a payment. The cleanest professional relationship Silas has. He suspects the Tyrant sees him as disposable. He doesn't care — all clients do. The smart ones just don't say it.
- **Necromancer** — Professional associate. Both deal in death, but differently. Silas finds the Necromancer's obsession with death-as-a-concept slightly theatrical. The Necromancer finds Silas's transactional approach to killing disappointingly mundane. They work well together anyway. Death doesn't care about philosophy.
- **Earth Warden** — The Warden knows Orin. The Warden probably knows what Silas was supposed to become. Silas avoids the Warden the way you avoid a mirror when you know you won't like what you see. The Warden's earth magic feels like Orin's — grounded, patient, *healing*. It makes Silas's skin crawl.
- **Arc Strider (Kaelen)** — A hunter recognizes another hunter. Kaelen chases storm-beasts. Silas chases people. Different prey, similar instincts. Kaelen is faster. Silas is quieter. Mutual respect, no friendship. Kaelen would buy him a drink. Silas would check the drink for poison out of habit.
- **Iron Knight (Garen)** — Everything Silas isn't. Honor, duty, self-sacrifice. Garen looks at Silas and sees a coward who kills from the shadows. Silas looks at Garen and sees a man who'll die for a principle that won't mourn him back. Neither is wrong.
- **Dawn Priest** — The living antidote. The Priest's light cleanses poison — including Silas's. Functionally, they cancel each other out. Philosophically, the Priest is what Silas could have been if he'd chosen the other face of every plant. Silas finds the Priest's compassion unbearable. Not because it's wrong, but because it reminds him of Orin.
- **Frost Queen (Eirwen)** — Cold and poison are natural partners. She freezes them in place, he poisons them while they can't move. Professionally elegant. She treats him like a tool, which is fine — he treats everyone like a client. He's one of the few people who's noticed the exhaustion beneath her composure. He hasn't mentioned it. An assassin knows when not to strike.

## Counters

- **Weak against:** Light-heavy teams that cleanse his poisons faster than he can apply them. High-defense tanks who shrug off his moderate base damage before the poison ramps. Burst heroes who kill him before Shadow Veil can save him — 95 DEF means one focused combo ends him. Cleanse abilities strip his entire damage model.
- **Strong against:** Low-defense targets who can't survive sustained poison pressure. Heroes who rely on long fights — Silas gets stronger the longer combat lasts through Lethal Dose stacking. Heroes without cleanse watch their HP drain tick by tick with no answer. Single-target duelists walk into his trap — stealth, poison, detonate.
