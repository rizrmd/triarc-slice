# Tenzin, the Wind Monk

## Lore

Tenzin was born in the stone guardian lineage — a tradition as old as the mountains themselves. The stone guardians are earthians who tend to the great sleeping spirits of the highlands: the mountain sentinels, the bedrock watchers, the ancient elemental beings who form the bones of the world. Tenzin's duty was to tend Bolverk — the oldest of them, a mountain spirit who had slept for millennia beneath the highland peaks.

For years, Tenzin did what every stone guardian does. He cleared the rockslides. He maintained the prayer cairns. He listened to the deep rumble of the earth's breathing and reported tremors to the elders. He was good at it — patient, steady, rooted. He knew every crevice of Bolverk's slopes, every mineral vein, every hollow where the mountain's heartbeat was loudest. He loved the stone. The stone was home.

Then the wind came.

Not a storm — a breath. Tenzin was meditating at Bolverk's summit, the highest point on the highland range, when the wind passed through him. Not around him. *Through* him. For an instant, he felt what it was to have no weight, no roots, no attachment to the ground beneath him. He felt the sky. He felt movement without destination, force without resistance, existence without form. And in that formless moment, he understood something the stone could never teach him: the earth holds. The wind *releases*.

He spent three more years on the mountain, but the stone knew he was leaving before he did. The elders weren't surprised. It's in the lineage — the stone guardian tradition has always produced wind monks. Most stay rooted. Some are called upward. The earth gives them their foundation, and then the wind gives them everything else. It's not a betrayal. It's the next step in a very old path.

Bolverk understood. Mountains don't judge, and Bolverk had felt guardians come and go for millennia. But something in the old spirit recognized Tenzin — the way he listened, the way he sat in silence, the way the wind around the summit changed when he was there. If a mountain could miss someone, Bolverk might miss Tenzin. He would never say so. Mountains don't say.

Tenzin descended from the peaks and wandered. He studied wind philosophy in monasteries across three kingdoms, trained with wandering martial artists, and slowly shed the weight of stone from his spirit. His fighting style emerged from this transition — rooted footwork (the earth in him) combined with flowing strikes and impossible evasion (the wind he became). He doesn't fight to win. He fights to *flow*. A fight that flows perfectly ends itself.

He doesn't fight in the war for a cause. He fights because the war is a blockage in the world's flow — violence, dominion, conquest, all of it is stagnation disguised as force. Severan's empire is the opposite of wind: a rigid structure that crushes everything beneath it. Tenzin doesn't hate the Tyrant. He simply moves through the spaces the Tyrant creates, and things break open behind him.

## Personality

Serene, present, gently amused. Tenzin speaks in observations, not commands. He notices things others miss — the way a fighter favors one leg, the way a commander's voice tightens before an order, the way a river changes direction without deciding to. He finds beauty in movement and sadness in stillness. He smiles often, laughs easily, and is almost impossible to provoke.

Underneath: a man who chose to let go of everything solid and is still learning what that means. He left the mountain, the stone, the only home he knew. He doesn't regret it — but some mornings, when the air is very still, he feels the weight of the earth in his bones and wonders if the wind was an answer or an escape. He has decided it doesn't matter. Flow doesn't ask why.

## Lore Relationships

- **Earth Warden (Bolverk)** — The mountain he once tended. Tenzin spent years on Bolverk's slopes — clearing rockslides, maintaining cairns, listening to the deep rumble of the earth breathing. He loved the stone. Then the wind called him away. Bolverk understood, the way mountains understand erosion: it's natural, it's inevitable, and it changes the shape of things. If they fight on the same field, the earth in Tenzin's bones pulls against the wind in his spirit, and Bolverk's stone cracks under the gust of someone who used to protect it. They cannot stand together. It is the saddest incompatibility in the roster.
- **Arc Strider (Kaelen)** — Kaelen is lightning — fast, violent, electric. Tenzin is breeze — flowing, patient, everywhere. Kaelen finds Tenzin's meditation boring. Tenzin finds Kaelen's recklessness charming. The monk's philosophy of flow appeals to Kaelen more than he'll ever admit. They fight well together because wind and lightning are siblings — different moods of the same sky.
- **Storm Ranger (Seryn)** — She learned more from watching him move than from years of formal training. His patience mirrors hers, though his is spiritual where hers is tactical. He sees in her the archer's version of flow — waiting for the perfect moment, then releasing. She's the only person who understands his philosophy through a different discipline.
- **Frost Queen (Eirwen)** — She controls. He releases. She compresses. He expands. They are philosophical opposites and deeply effective allies. She finds him infuriating — his serenity feels like a judgment of her rigidity. He finds her sad — all that control, all that composure, and he can see the cracks she's holding together. He might be the only person who sees Eirwen clearly. She resents that. She needs it.
- **Flame Warlock (Malachar)** — Wind feeds fire, but Tenzin's wind refuses to serve Malachar's chaos. The monk sees Malachar as a corruption of elemental truth — fire given to madness instead of purpose. Malachar's flame disrupts Tenzin's flow the way a bonfire disrupts a breeze. The monk pities him. Malachar would burn the monk for that pity.
- **Dawn Priest** — Warmth and wind. The Priest's light doesn't burn — it guides. Tenzin finds the Priest's compassion genuine in a way that most people's compassion isn't. They share a belief that the world can be healed, though they disagree on whether healing requires stillness (the Priest) or movement (the Monk). Friendly arguments that never resolve and never need to.
- **Tyrant Overlord (Severan)** — Everything Tenzin left behind. Rigidity, weight, the need to hold and control. Severan is earth without wisdom — domination without understanding. Tenzin doesn't hate him. Hatred is a root, and roots are weight. He simply moves through the spaces Severan's empire creates, and the walls crack behind him.
