# Morvain, the Necromancer

## Lore

Morvain was a scholar before he was a necromancer. He studied at the same academy as Malachar — different discipline, same obsession. Where Malachar wanted to free fire, Morvain wanted to understand death. Not to prevent it or cause it — to comprehend it. What happens in the moment between alive and not? Where does the self go? Can it be recalled?

His colleagues called it morbid. He called it the most important question anyone had ever asked. When the academy wouldn't fund his research, he funded it himself — in graveyards, in crypts, in battlefields after the fighting stopped. He learned to speak to the dead, then to command them, then to walk the border between life and death himself.

He didn't fall to darkness. He walked into it with a lantern, taking notes the entire way. His body withered — the lowest HP in the roster — because he's given most of his living vitality to fuel his research. He's more dead than alive, and he considers this acceptable data.

Vexara considers him a lieutenant. He considers her a fascinating subject — an entity that exists beyond death. She doesn't know he's studying her as much as serving her. Malachar is the only person who understands Morvain's obsession, because Malachar has his own. Two scholars who went too far, standing at opposite ends of the same madness.

## Personality

Clinical, obsessive, theatrical. Morvain speaks about death the way a biologist speaks about cells — with genuine fascination and zero emotional weight. He narrates his own rituals. He catalogues the dying. He is not cruel — cruelty requires malice, and Morvain has none. He has curiosity, and curiosity doesn't care about feelings.

Underneath: a man who has spent so long studying death that he's forgotten how to be alive. He doesn't eat for pleasure, doesn't sleep for rest, doesn't talk for connection. Everything is research. The loneliness isn't a wound — it's a variable he stopped measuring years ago.

## Lore Relationships

- **Flame Warlock (Malachar)** — Academy brothers. They studied side by side before they each walked into their respective obsessions. Malachar reached for fire. Morvain reached for death. They understand each other in the way that only two people consumed by the same kind of madness can. They don't judge each other. There's no one left who could.
- **Demon Empress (Vexara)** — She considers him a lieutenant. He considers her a subject. An entity that exists beyond death — the most fascinating case study he's ever encountered. He serves her because proximity is data. She tolerates him because competence is useful. Neither knows the other's true agenda. Both suspect. That he also serves Severan's wars for the steady supply of corpses doesn't bother her — as long as the research flows upward. It doesn't. But she hasn't noticed yet.
- **Night Venom (Silas)** — Professional associates. Both deal in death, but differently. Morvain finds Silas's transactional approach to killing disappointingly mundane. Silas finds Morvain's obsession with death-as-a-concept slightly theatrical. They work well together anyway. Death doesn't care about philosophy.
- **Dawn Priest (Auryn)** — His opposite in every way. She reaches into light, he reaches into death. She heals, he drains. She grieves for him — "he was someone's child once." He finds her compassion theoretically interesting and practically irrelevant. She is the one person who could potentially pull him back. He doesn't want to be pulled back.
- **Arcane Paladin (Aldric)** — A dark mirror. Aldric reached into light the way Morvain reached into death. Same devotion, opposite direction. Aldric doesn't hate him. He pities him. Morvain finds pity far more offensive than hatred.
- **Iron Knight (Garen)** — Garen protects the living. Morvain studies the dead. There is no overlap, no understanding, no common ground. Garen looks at Morvain and sees everything worth fighting against. Morvain looks at Garen and sees a future research subject.
- **Spellblade Empress (Mirael)** — Their other peer from the Academy. She found balance where Malachar and Morvain found obsession. She considers both of them personal failures. Morvain considers her a cautionary tale in the other direction — someone who learned everything and understood nothing deeply enough. She reached for breadth. He reached for depth. She became a headmistress. He became a corpse that still takes notes.
- **Blood Alchemist** — A colleague in the manipulation of vitality. The Blood Alchemist transmutes life. Morvain transcribes death. Different chapters of the same forbidden textbook. They share notes. Figuratively. Sometimes literally.
